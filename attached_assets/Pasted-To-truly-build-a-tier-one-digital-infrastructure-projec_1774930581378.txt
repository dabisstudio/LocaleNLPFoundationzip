Here is the architectural deep dive into the **Sustaining Supporter Dashboard**, designed to maximize retention and drive upsells by making the user's impact undeniably concrete.

### 1️⃣ The Psychology of the Impact Portfolio
The goal of this dashboard is to instantly answer the donor's subconscious question: *"What exactly did my money do today?"*

We achieve this by abandoning abstract mission statements in favor of hard, undeniable metrics.

* **The Hero Metric (The ROI):** Front and center, the user sees a single, massive number representing their direct output. For LocaleNLP, this shouldn't be "Dollars Donated." It should be "Validated Speech Hours Funded" or "Tokens Processed." This translates currency directly into infrastructure.
* **The Allocation Ledger (The Proof):** Institutional donors demand granular transparency. A sleek, interactive visualization must break down their specific contribution into operational categories (Annotators, Compute, Fellowships), proving extreme capital efficiency.
* **The Live Field Feed (The Connection):** To maintain emotional resonance alongside the hard data, the dashboard features a localized activity feed. This pulls in anonymized, real-time updates from the Lughatna platform (e.g., "500 Bambara audio clips validated in Bamako"). It makes the infrastructure feel alive.

### 2️⃣ Architectural Requirements
To execute this seamlessly, your engineering team will need to connect your payment processor (Stripe) with your backend database (where Lughatna activity is logged).

1.  **Stripe Webhooks:** When a recurring donation clears, Stripe pings your backend.
2.  **The Impact Algorithm:** Your backend calculates the proportional impact of that specific dollar amount based on your current operational costs (e.g., if it costs $10 to process 1 hour of speech, a $100 donation credits the user with 10 hours).
3.  **GraphQL Delivery:** The frontend (Next.js) queries this specific user's aggregated impact data and renders the dashboard instantly upon login.
