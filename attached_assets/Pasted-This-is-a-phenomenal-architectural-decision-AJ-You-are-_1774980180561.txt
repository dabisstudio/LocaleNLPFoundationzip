### 1️⃣ THE CANVAS (The Institutional Whites)
We avoid pure digital white (`#FFFFFF`) for the main background. Pure white hurts the eyes and feels like a cheap template. We use colors that feel like thick, expensive paper or carved stone.

* **Foundation Stone (Main Background): `#F5F5F3`** * *The Vibe:* A cool, grounded alabaster. It feels permanent, historical, and highly editorial. It immediately signals that this is a serious institution, not a startup.
* **Pristine White (Elevated Components): `#FFFFFF`**
    * *The Application:* Use pure white *only* for the floating SaaS cards (like the Data Explorer or Pricing Tiers) resting on top of the Foundation Stone background. This creates crisp, sharp contrast without needing heavy shadows.
* **Archival Grey (Subtle Backgrounds): `#EAEAEA`**
    * *The Application:* For secondary sections (like the footer or the governance ledger) to create architectural separation.

### 2️⃣ THE INK (The Typography of Power)
To make an off-white site look bold and authoritative, your typography must provide massive contrast. 

* **Monument Black (Primary Headings): `#0C0C0C`**
    * *The Vibe:* This is not pure black (`#000000`), which can look harsh. This is a deep, rich charcoal. When used on massive, heavy typography (like *Space Grotesk* or *Inter Tight*), it feels like heavy lead ink pressed into the page. It commands absolute attention.
* **Steel Slate (Body Text & Metadata): `#4A4A56`**
    * *The Vibe:* Clinical, highly legible, and sophisticated. It keeps the reading experience frictionless for researchers reviewing long documentation.

### 3️⃣ THE POWER ACCENTS (Heritage & Momentum)
This is where you inject the identity of the foundation. You are building datasets for African and Arabic languages. The accents should nod to this heritage, but in a premium, highly restrained way.

* **The Sovereign Ochre (Primary Action): `#D95C14`**
    * *The Vibe:* A bold, burnt terracotta/ochre. It evokes the earth, clay, and vitality of the African continent, but it is saturated enough to look like a modern tech accent. Use this for your primary "Fund a Bounty" or "Donate" buttons. It pops violently and beautifully against the off-white stone.
* **The Trust Navy (Secondary Action & Badges): `#0A1931`**
    * *The Vibe:* Deep, unshakeable institutional trust. Use this for trust badges, "Verified 501(c)(3)" tags, or the "Enterprise API ↗" button that links out to your For-Profit site. It bridges the gap between the foundation's warmth and the enterprise's security.
* **Validation Green (Data & Success): `#0F763D`**
    * *The Vibe:* A sharp, emerald green used strictly for positive data indicators on the Live Vitality Index or when a contributor successfully validates a token.

### ⚡ HOW TO MAKE OFF-WHITE LOOK "BOLD" (UI Rules)

Color is only half the battle. To ensure the Foundation site feels powerful and not passive, your UI design must follow these rules:

1.  **Brutal Typography Scaling:** Don't be shy with text size. Your H1s should be massive and tightly kerned (letters close together). A massive black headline on an alabaster background is the ultimate flex of confidence.
2.  **Sharp Edges, Not Soft Bubbles:** The Circula aesthetic uses very soft, rounded corners. To inject more *power* into the LocaleNLP Foundation, reduce the border radius slightly. Instead of `24px` pill-shapes, use `8px` or `12px` rounded corners. It feels slightly more engineered and architectural.
3.  **Hairline Borders:** Outline your white UI cards with a razor-thin, high-contrast border (e.g., `1px solid #D1D1D1`). This is a hallmark of elite editorial design (think *The New York Times* or high-end fintech platforms). It gives structure to the whitespace.
4.  **The "Dark Mode" Inversion for the Footer:** To create a visual anchor at the bottom of the page, make the entire footer area the exact same dark color as your For-Profit site. This creates a massive visual weight at the bottom, grounding the off-white page, and visually hinting at the deep-tech engine powering the foundation.
