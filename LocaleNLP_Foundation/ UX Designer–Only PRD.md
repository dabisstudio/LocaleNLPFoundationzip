# 11️⃣ DESIGN SYSTEM

*(Components, Tokens, Usage Rules)*

The design system is **not a branding exercise**.
It is a **governance mechanism** ensuring consistency, accessibility, and institutional credibility across all touchpoints.

---

## 11.1 Design System Philosophy

The LocaleNLP design system must:

* Reduce cognitive load
* Prevent stylistic drift
* Encode ethics and accessibility into defaults
* Be boring in the best possible way

> If a component feels “flashy,” it is wrong.

---

## 11.2 Design Tokens

### 11.2.1 Color Tokens

**Purpose:** Orientation, hierarchy, state — never decoration.

#### Categories

* Neutral (primary)
* Accent (secondary, limited)
* Semantic (status only)

**Rules**

* Color is never the only signal
* Accent colors are used sparingly
* Semantic colors only for system states

**Prohibited**

* Gradients for meaning
* Saturated colors for CTAs
* Color-coded persuasion

---

### 11.2.2 Typography Tokens

**Primary Goals**

* Readability
* Cross-script compatibility
* Institutional tone

#### Hierarchy

* Display (rare)
* Headings (H1–H4)
* Body
* Small / Meta

**Rules**

* Line length optimized for reading
* Clear contrast ratios
* No stylized display fonts

---

### 11.2.3 Spacing & Layout Tokens

Spacing communicates **respect and calm**.

* Consistent vertical rhythm
* Generous whitespace
* Predictable grid

**Anti-Pattern**

* Dense information blocks
* “Everything above the fold” mentality

---

## 11.3 Core Components

### 11.3.1 Buttons

**Types**

* Primary (1 per screen)
* Secondary
* Tertiary (text-only)

**Rules**

* Buttons explain actions clearly
* No urgency language
* Disabled states must explain why

---

### 11.3.2 Cards

Used for:

* Programs
* Articles
* Impact stories

**Rules**

* Flat, minimal elevation
* Clear heading + summary
* Entire card clickable OR explicit CTA (never both)

---

### 11.3.3 Navigation Components

* Top navigation (global)
* Breadcrumbs (deep pages)
* Footer navigation (trust layer)

**Rule**
Navigation must never feel “hidden” or clever.

---

### 11.3.4 Informational Components

* Callouts
* Notices
* Disclaimers
* Expandable sections

**Usage**

* Explain complexity
* Surface ethics
* Clarify consent

---

# 12️⃣ UX WRITING & CONTENT STRATEGY GUIDE

Content is **part of the UX**, not decoration.

---

## 12.1 Voice Principles

* Calm
* Respectful
* Precise
* Globally intelligible

**Never**

* Promotional
* Overconfident
* Technical without explanation

---

## 12.2 Reading Level & Language

* Target: Plain language
* Short sentences
* Active voice
* Avoid idioms

**If a sentence needs rereading, rewrite it.**

---

## 12.3 Content Hierarchy

Every page must clearly separate:

1. Core message
2. Supporting explanation
3. Optional depth

This supports **progressive disclosure**.

---

## 12.4 Microcopy Rules

Microcopy must:

* Reduce anxiety
* Clarify expectations
* Reinforce trust

**Examples**

❌ “Submit data now”
✅ “Share language data (you can stop at any time)”

---

## 12.5 Ethical Content Requirements

Every page involving:

* Data
* AI
* Participation

Must include:

* Purpose explanation
* Consent clarity
* Link to governance

# 15️⃣ ACCESSIBILITY-FIRST DESIGN CHECKLIST

Designers must verify **before handoff**:

### Visual

* Color contrast compliant
* Text resizable
* No color-only meaning

### Interaction

* Keyboard navigable
* Focus visible
* Logical tab order

### Motion

* Reduced motion supported
* No essential meaning conveyed by animation

### Content

* Clear headings
* Descriptive links
* Plain language

Accessibility is **a design requirement**, not a QA step.

# 16️⃣ DESIGN CRITIQUE & QA RUBRIC

This rubric is used for **internal reviews**.

---

## 16.1 Trust & Ethics

* Is intent clear?
* Is consent explicit?
* Is governance visible?

---

## 16.2 Clarity

* Can a non-expert explain the page?
* Is jargon explained?
* Is hierarchy obvious?

---

## 16.3 Inclusion

* Does this work on low bandwidth?
* Is language globally understandable?
* Are accessibility needs respected?

---

## 16.4 Consistency

* Does this align with the design system?
* Are components reused correctly?
* Is tone consistent?

---

## 16.5 Restraint

* Is anything unnecessary?
* Is motion justified?
* Is decoration overpowering meaning?
